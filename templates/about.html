About us

Our web application leverages TensorFlow and advanced machine learning algorithms to assist in the early detection of skin cancer. By analyzing uploaded images of skin lesions, the system identifies potential signs of melanoma and other skin cancers. With a user-friendly interface, it enables quick image uploads and delivers instant, accurate results. The app is built to ensure user privacy and secure handling of data. Our aim is to provide a reliable tool that uses AI technology to support early diagnosis, improving treatment outcomes through timely detection.

General Information About Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is a disease in which malignant (cancer) cells form in the tissues of the skin.

Skin cancer is the most common cancer in the United States.

Different factors increase or decrease the risk of skin cancer.

Types of Skin Cancer

There are two main types of skin cancer:

Nonmelanoma, also called keratinocyte carcinoma, includes squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) and basal cell carcinoma.

Melanoma is more likely to spread and harder to cure if not detected early.

Skin Cancer Screening

Screening for skin cancer may include examination by both the patient and the health care provider.

Regular self-examinations and dermatologist check-ups are important.

If an area on the skin looks abnormal, a biopsy is usually done.

Risks of Skin Cancer Screening

Finding skin cancer does not always improve health or help you live longer.

False-negative test results can give a false sense of security.

False-positive test results can lead to unnecessary tests and anxiety.

Biopsies may cause scarring or infection.

Talk to your doctor about your risk for skin cancer and your need for screening tests.
